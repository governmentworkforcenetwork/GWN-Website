U.S. Career Center Network

A growing education and workforce visibility network.

This directory reflects universities, colleges, and career centers where Government Workforce Network LLC has received approval to engage, conduct outreach, post opportunities, create events, support career education, participate in career fairs, or expand workforce visibility.

The purpose is to help students, alumni, veterans, career seekers, and workforce partners access practical tools, educational sessions, visibility opportunities, and Talent Connect resources.

Global Workforce Learning Exchange

Cross-border learning. Real workforce conversations.

Government Workforce Network LLC is expanding university engagement through the Global Workforce Learning Exchange, a free workforce learning and engagement program designed to connect students, alumni, universities, employers, workforce professionals, veterans, and community leaders across regions.

This program complements existing university career services, alumni engagement, student success, and workforce readiness programs.

Connecting students, universities, and workforce leaders across borders

Founding Global University Program

We are currently inviting universities outside the United States to participate in our Founding Global University Program. Initial outreach includes Canada, the United Kingdom, India, the Philippines, Australia, and other regions as the network grows.

Free Opportunities

For participating institutions

  • Access to Workforce TV educational discussions
  • Access to Virtual Workforce Lounges
  • Student and alumni workforce engagement opportunities
  • Leadership and hands-on workforce learning projects
  • Cross-border learning with students and professionals from other regions
  • Student, alumni, and university spotlight opportunities
  • Discussions focused on AI, leadership, workforce readiness, recruitment, and the future of work

Participation is free. No membership fees, no contractual obligations, and no administrative burden.

Important Note: Approval to engage is not the same as formal sponsorship, endorsement, affiliation, or contractual partnership with each institution.
